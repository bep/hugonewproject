---
title: "On reading old code"
date: 2026-04-08
---

There is a particular kind of patience required to read a codebase you wrote three years ago. You do not get to lean on intent. The reasons that seemed obvious at the time are not obvious anymore, and the comments you left are written for someone you used to be.

The temptation is to refactor on first reading. Resist that. Read the whole thing first. Take notes if you need to. The shape of the system reveals itself slowly, and the parts that look ugliest are usually the ones that took the most thought.

I have started to think of old code less as something I wrote and more as something I inherited. It is a kinder framing. It also tends to produce better commits.
